What is mantu?

Mantu is a traditional Afghan dish that consists of savory dumplings filled with spiced meat. These delightful parcels are typically served with a tangy yogurt sauce and a drizzle of tomato sauce, creating a harmonious blend of flavors. Mantu is a beloved dish in Afghan cuisine, often enjoyed on special occasions and family gatherings.

Here's a simple recipe to make Afghan Mantu:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground tumeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wonton or dumbling wrappers

For the yogurt sauce:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

For the tomato sauce:

  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste

    • Instructions:

    1. In a bowl, mix together the ground meat, chopped onion, minced garlic, coriander, cumin, turmeric, salt, and pepper until well combined.
    2. Place a small spoonful of the meat mixture in the center of each dumpling wrapper. Fold the wrapper over the filling and pinch the edges to seal, creating a dumpling shape.
    3. Steam the dumplings in a steamer basket for about 15-20 minutes, or until the meat is cooked through and the wrappers are tender.

    For the yogurt sauce:

    1. In a small bowl, mix together the plain yogurt, minced garlic, and salt. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.

    For the tomato sauce:

    1. Heat the vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the ground cumin and cook for a minute until fragrant.
    2. Pour in the tomato puree and season with salt. Simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.

    To serve:

    Place the steamed mantu dumplings on a plate and drizzle with the yogurt sauce and tomato sauce. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs, such as cilantro or mint, if desired. Serve hot and enjoy the delicious flavors of Afghan mantu!
